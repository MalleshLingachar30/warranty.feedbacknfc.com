# Platform Positioning Note

## Core Positioning

This platform should not be positioned as a generic warranty registration tool. Its strongest and clearest positioning is as an **installation-activated warranty and post-sales traceability platform** for manufacturers of serialized equipment.

The platform is most relevant where warranty should begin when a product is actually installed or commissioned, not merely when it is dispatched into the channel.

## What The Platform Is

The platform is a post-sales operations layer that connects:

- serialized main products
- serviceable spare parts
- installation activation
- end-customer and site capture
- complaint and service workflow
- technician execution
- spare replacement proof
- customer acknowledgement
- service history and accountability

It gives manufacturers a connected operational view of what happens after a product leaves central inventory and enters the dealer, distributor, installer, technician, and customer ecosystem.

## What The Platform Is Not

The platform is not best positioned as:

- only a warranty card app
- only a QR complaint app
- only a customer registration app
- only a service desk replacement
- only an SAP integration layer

Those may be part of the solution, but they do not describe the real operational value.

## Ideal Customer Profile

The platform has the strongest fit with manufacturers that have most of the following characteristics:

- products are serialized
- products move first through dealers or distributors
- end customer is often unknown at dispatch
- installation or commissioning is mandatory or important
- warranty should start from installation, activation, or commissioning
- service is field-driven and technician-led
- spare replacement must be controlled
- old-part return matters
- customer/site traceability matters
- audit trail and service history matter

## Best-Fit Industries

The clearest-fit industries are:

- medical technology manufacturers
- diagnostic equipment manufacturers
- hospital equipment manufacturers
- industrial machinery manufacturers
- heavy equipment manufacturers
- laboratory equipment manufacturers
- commercial equipment manufacturers
- water treatment and purification equipment manufacturers
- HVAC and infrastructure equipment manufacturers
- energy and electrical equipment manufacturers where commissioning matters

## The Problem This Platform Solves

Many manufacturers have visibility up to dispatch or invoicing, but lose operational traceability after products move into the channel.

Typical gaps include:

- final customer not known at dispatch
- warranty start date not aligned to installation
- handwritten or delayed installation reporting
- disconnected service and complaint handling
- weak linkage between spare issuance and actual field replacement
- poor visibility into old-part return
- no dependable customer acknowledgement of completed service
- no unified operational interface between manufacturer, dealer, technician, and customer

The platform is designed to close these gaps.

## Operating Model The Platform Supports

The strongest workflow the platform supports is:

1. Main product is serialized and tagged with QR/NFC identity
2. Dealer or installer performs digital installation activation
3. Customer and site details are captured at installation
4. Warranty starts from installation date
5. Complaint is registered against the installed unit
6. Technician is assigned and accepts the job
7. Spare parts are issued against a specific ticket
8. Technician scans both main product QR and spare QR during replacement
9. Customer acknowledges service completion
10. Old-part return and closure are tracked through the same chain

This creates a complete proof chain from installation to service resolution.

## Plug-and-Play vs Installation-Required Positioning

The platform should support two operational modes:

### Plug-and-Play Mode

Used where products can be used immediately and installation is not mandatory.

Typical use:

- simpler appliances
- low-complexity products
- standard retail products

### Installation-Required Mode

Used where commissioning or installation is essential.

Typical use:

- medical equipment
- heavy equipment
- capital goods
- complex serviceable equipment

This mode is the strongest positioning for the platform and should be the main sales anchor for the right industries.

## Recommended Market Message

The clearest message is:

> This is an installation-activated warranty and post-sales traceability platform for serialized equipment manufacturers.

An extended version:

> The platform helps manufacturers connect serialized products, installation activation, customer/site capture, service tickets, technician workflow, spare replacement, and customer acknowledgement into one auditable post-sales operating system.

## How To Position Against Existing Systems

### SAP

SAP remains the commercial and inventory system of record.

The platform does not need to replace SAP. Its role is to connect commercial dispatch to real-world installation, service execution, and spare traceability.

### Service Desk / Legacy Service Software

If an existing service desk works well, the platform does not need to duplicate every service function immediately. Instead, it can become the operational traceability and field-proof layer around the existing process.

## Strategic Sales Advantage

This positioning gives the platform a stronger enterprise story:

- faster justification than a simple warranty product
- higher relevance to operations, service, and compliance teams
- stronger value in dealer-driven markets
- better fit for serialized and installed equipment
- clearer ROI through reduced leakage, better traceability, and cleaner service closure

## Bottom Line

The platform is best positioned as a **post-sales control layer for installed and serviceable equipment**, especially where warranty starts from installation and spare replacement must be fully traceable.

The strongest commercial focus should be:

- medical equipment manufacturers
- heavy equipment manufacturers
- other serialized, installed, service-intensive product businesses
